The Indian independence movement was in constant ideological evolution. Essentially anti-colonial, it was supplemented by visions of independent
, economic development with a secular, democratic, republican, and civil-libertarian political structure. After the 1930s, the movement took
on a strong socialist orientation. It culminated in the Indian Independence Act 1947, which ended Crown suzerainty and partitioned
British Raj into Dominion of India and Dominion of Pakistan India remained a Crown Dominion until 26 January 1950, when the Constitution of
India established the Republic of India. Pakistan remained a dominion until 1956 when it adopted its first constitution. In 1971, East Pakistan
declared its own independence as Bangladesh.
